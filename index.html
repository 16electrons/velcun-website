The Problem

Freight software is reactive. The market needs autonomous execution.

As carriers shift from aggressive cost-cutting to active margin manufacturing, severe structural gaps have emerged in standard freight software.

Reactive Dashboards

Most software shows where you lost money last week. Decision intelligence for tomorrow's margin is missing.

Siloed Systems

TMS, ELD, payments, and border data don't talk. Each system operates in isolation, creating operational blind spots.

Manual Back-Office

Hours spent copy-pasting from PDFs, emails, and WhatsApp into TMS. No semantic ingestion for freight-specific data.

Payment Delays

30-day payment cycles, factoring fees, and manual audits. No machine-to-machine settlement infrastructure.

Driver Surveillance

Apps track compliance and harsh braking, but don't optimize for predictable income, home-time, or retention.

Opportunity Layers

The high-leverage middleware standard freight software missed

Most freight platforms report what happened. VELCUN is designed to verify, decide, and execute across the 5 operational gaps that still drain carrier margin.

Agentic Settlement Layer

The Gap: Existing billing software is rules-based and slow. Brokers delay payments, factoring companies charge heavy fees, and there's no machine-to-machine financial infrastructure.

The Solution: Escrow funds, verify digital PoD via telematics geofencing, and trigger autonomous carrier payments instantly when criteria are met—no 30-day delays or back-office rekeying.

Predictive Lane Optimization

The Gap: Current systems show beautiful dashboards of past metrics but lack decision intelligence. Carriers don't need to see where they lost money last week—they need to know how to maximize margins tomorrow.

The Solution: Analyze real-time variables—weather shifts, receiver delays, port congestion, capacity changes—to recommend fleet positioning before market shifts occur.

Unstructured Data Ingestion

The Gap: Freight runs on fragmented communication—PDFs, emails, WhatsApp chats. Shippers spend hours manually copy-pasting data from rate confirmations into proprietary TMS platforms.

The Solution: Verticalized semantic rewriting engine that extracts complex multi-stop routing and accessorial structures from unstructured text/PDFs, verifies against contracts, and pushes clean API payloads into legacy TMS.

Driver-First Retention Tools

The Gap: Driver turnover nears 90%. Most apps treat drivers as telemetry points (tracking harsh braking, speeding) rather than optimizing for satisfaction or predictable income.

The Solution: Optimization engine that weights driver preference and volatile pay structures alongside fuel efficiency—designing routes around guaranteed weekly pay and home-time while balancing fleet profitability.

Multi-Modal Border Compliance

The Gap: Software is fragmented by mode or country. Domestic TMS rarely communicates with customs databases or rail systems. Cross-border freight lacks unified execution layers.

The Solution: Unified infrastructure that synchronizes customs declarations, asset tracking, and multi-modal handoffs—automatically flagging paperwork anomalies 12 hours before border checkpoints and recalculating downstream legs.
